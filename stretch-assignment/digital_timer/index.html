Time Perception

Perception of time varies based on mental state. For example, some studies suggest that low doses of caffeine improve prospective time estimation in people who typically consume caffeine, but in people who do not, caffeine produces shorter time estimates (e.g., thinking 3 seconds have passed when in actuality 5 have). This is contrary to the hypothesis that caffeine would "increase the internal clock rate" and cause longer estimates.

Try starting the timer, closing your eyes, and counting to ten before opening your eyes. Do you overestimate, or underestimate the passage of time?
